Design Mobile Preview Experience

SlideShare had been a dominant content publishing platform for presentations, documents etc since it was founded in October 2006. The primary use case in the early days was web/desktop ofcourse, but trend had been changing with a fast & steady growth of mobile users over the years. SlideShare knew that mobile is going to be at the forefront of content consumption and felt the need to make the content creators aware of this fact.

We went ahead and started brainstorming on how this can be done. We wanted to simulate a realistic mobile app interface where the authors could see how their content looked on that smaller screen. The idea was to show the actual mobile app in an iframe. We thought this would help them see the flaws and eventually get better at creating mobile-friendly slides. I started exploring different possibilities for the same and came up with some wireframes and mockups.

User Study Findings

I started doing user study and shared the mockups with users from varied background and skills. It turned out that a lot of users did not the notice the switches on right hand side of the preview bar on top of the page. Also, navigating through the slides was not very intuitive so I began to include the findings into my next design iteration. It was also concluded that we should also add landscape orientation becasue of the fact that landscape orientation on SlideShare renders the best viewing experience.

After another round of user study and further changes, I started working on the front end implementation. I was responsible of HTML, css and basic javascript to launch preview and change the devices or orientation. I added CSS3 animations (used LESS CSS) to rotate the devices when changing orientation from landscape to portrait (and vice-versa) and transition from mobile device to tablet device.

While implementating, we came across another challenge. Web browsers on certain operating systems (read windows) did not have enough height to accomodate the orienatation switch at the bottom. Since sclaing down the device size was not acceptable I made another design change. What you see below is the final iteration which made it to the SlideShare website eventually. This design works perfectly on all operating systems and web browsers.
