SlideShare Upload Page Redesign

Uploads had always been one of the key drivers fueling SlideShare's growth. Essentially, this is where a user with an intent to publish content on SlideShare interacts with the interface.

The upload page had long suffered with an unsolved design challenge. Many first time users would get confused between the two call-to-actions to upload content — an orange button meant for free users, and a blue button (upload+) meant for PRO users to upload large files, videos and private content. Post SlideShare acquisition, LinkedIn wanted to offer Slideshare PRO features to all users for free. At the same time, SlideShare was also exploring various content creation opportunites. This is when we started thinking about the redesign.

The primary objectives of the redesign

  • Drop the Upload+ button.
  • Show public/private option for both free and PRO users.
  • Add CTAs for content creation apps: Haiku Deck and Professional Journey.
  • Support upload for all cloud services like Dropbox, Box, GDrive, Gmail etc.
  • Require drag-and-drop functionality to upload.
  • Reinforce various content types that users can upload other than presentations.

I started with discussing the requirements with the product partner on our team. I created a few paper sketches and later turned them into wireframes using wireframing tool Balsamiq. Below are a couple of wireframes:

After collecting feedback from the design and product teams, we finalized on the above design. Finally, I started working on the implementation of the page and collaborated with an engineer and quality assurance teams to succesfully roll out the redesigned functionality. There was a marginal increase in the number of uploads after the redesign but we managed to increase the upload-via-cloud numbers significantly. Combined with the introduction of new content creation CTAs, it proved to be a clear win-win for SlideShare.
